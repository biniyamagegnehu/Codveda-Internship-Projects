About Ethiopia

Discover the land of origins and diversity

A Rich Tapestry of Culture

Ethiopia, often called the "cradle of humanity," is one of the oldest countries in the world. With over 80 ethnic groups and languages, it's a remarkable mosaic of cultures, traditions, and histories.

Languages

Ethiopia has over 80 languages spoken across its regions. Amharic is the official language, while Oromo, Tigrinya, and Somali are also widely spoken. English is taught in schools and used in business.

Traditions

From the unique Ethiopian calendar (7-8 years behind the Western calendar) to the traditional coffee ceremony, Ethiopian traditions are deeply rooted in community and hospitality.

Religion

Ethiopia is home to ancient religious traditions, including Ethiopian Orthodox Christianity (one of the oldest Christian denominations) and Islam, living together in harmony for centuries.
